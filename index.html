About

Biography

Vahid Taremi is an Iranian composer and a skilled player of the traditional Iranian instrument Shurangiz. He was born in 1987 and has established himself as a notable figure in the world of Persian music.

Taremi pursued his education at the Islamic Azad University, Tehran North Branch, where he earned both a Bachelor's and a Master's degree in Persian Literature. Additionally, he attended the Tehran Conservatory of Music from 2006 to 2009, focusing on playing Iranian instruments.

Throughout his career, Taremi has gained extensive teaching experience. He has taught at various institutions, including Farhang & Honar University 46, Shahid Beheshti University, Tarbiat Modares University, Nafir Artistic and Cultural Institute, Javan Cultural Center, and the Center of Open Courses / Soureh Institute of Graduate Studies.

As a composer, Taremi has published several notable compositions. In 2019, he contributed to the music album Little Black Fish as a composer, released by the HaftCraft label in Belgium. In 2016, he improvised a piece for Khayyam Khani released by RAHA Records. His other compositions include Shahre Sangestan (2012) and Khan-e-Hashtom (2010), where he served as composer and conductor. He was also involved in the collaborative project Self-Portrait in 2009.

Vahid's musical accomplishments extend well beyond his composition work. Notable highlights include a Shurangiz and Setar duet with Behdad Babaei in Berlin in 2025, and a trio concert at the Aga Khan Centre in London in 2025, alongside Behdad Babaei on Setar and Majid Khalaj on Tombak. In 2025, he also performed across Europe with the Shurangiz–Setar–Tombak ensemble: London (Rich Mix Festival, 21 November), Manchester (22 November), Sheffield (23 November), Paris (30 November), Berlin (6 December), and Ghent — De Centrale Hall (13 December).

In 2023, Vahid performed as Shurangiz soloist in The Phoenix at the Oxford International Song Festival at the Holywell Music Room. In 2021, he was featured in the Maah-o-Nava online festival. In 2020, he was part of the Sipa Festival in Jakarta as a Shurangiz player with the Triangle Ensemble. In 2019, he performed at the Glasperlenspiel Festival in Tartu, Estonia, as part of the Haftcraft Band. His international performances span Belgium, China, the United Kingdom, and Iran.

Vahid has made significant contributions to radio, television, and cinema — serving as music critic and analyzer on programs including Arghanoun, Karaneh, and Taranom, and composing scores for films such as A Report from Manaslu (2015), The Deteriorating World of Natia (2013), and Door Masho (2014), as well as documentaries and TV series.

His contributions also extend to theater, with compositions for productions including Afsoone Parizad (2009), Shab Kouche Eshgh (2008), Az o Be Yek Eshare (2007), Setare Kouchouloo (2007), and Istgah (2006).

Vahid Taremi's versatile skills as a composer, instrumentalist, teacher, and collaborator have established him as a prominent figure in Persian music, contributing to its richness and preservation.
